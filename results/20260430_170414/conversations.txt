This is a fantastic plot point! Criminals, even intelligent ones, often make surprisingly basic forensic errors due to panic, lack of knowledge, or underestimating the persistence of forensic science.

Here are some common mistakes, categorized for clarity:

#### I. Underestimating Trace Evidence

1.  **Leaving Behind DNA (The "Invisible" Killer):**
    *   **Mistake:** Believing that wiping down surfaces or submerging a body will destroy all DNA.

Criminals often forget to clean under fingernails, inside pockets, or the soles of shoes. Even secondary transfer (DNA from the victim transferred to the killer, then to an object) is possible.

### Common Mistakes Criminals Make When Attempting to Hide a Body (Forensic Realism) - *Continued*

#### II. Miscalculating Disposal Methods - *Continued*

4.  **Chemical Dissolution (The "Acid Bath" Fantasy):**
    *   **Mistake:** Assuming, often from pulp fiction, that a body can be quickly and completely dissolved in a bathtub or barrel using readily available chemicals like strong acids.
    *   **Reality:** While certain highly corrosive industrial chemicals (e.g., concentrated hydrofluoric acid, sulfuric acid, or strong bases like lye) can dissolve organic matter, this process is far more difficult, dangerous, and incomplete than often portrayed.
        *   **Quantity:** It requires an enormous volume and concentration of chemicals to dissolve an entire human body, especially bones.
        *   **Time:** It takes a very long time, often days or even weeks, even with agitation and heat.
        *   **Safety:** The chemicals themselves are extremely hazardous, producing noxious fumes and posing severe risks to the perpetrator.
        *   **Residuals:** Even if most tissues dissolve, teeth and bone fragments are notoriously resistant. Fatty tissues can saponify rather than fully dissolve.
    *   **How Investigators Exploit It:**
        *   **Chemical Residues:** The chemicals used leave distinct residues that can be detected in the container, on surrounding surfaces, or even in the soil if dumped.
        *   **Corrosion Damage:** The chemicals will corrode the container (e.g., a bathtub, metal barrel) and any plumbing they contact, leaving tell-tale signs.
        *   **Fragmentary Remains:** Almost invariably, some bone fragments, teeth, or even hair will remain. These can be recovered and analyzed for DNA or identification.
        *   **Unique Odors:** The chemical reactions produce very specific and often pungent odors that can linger.
        *   **Purchase Records:** The sheer volume of industrial-strength chemicals required would likely leave a paper trail (purchase records, delivery manifests).
